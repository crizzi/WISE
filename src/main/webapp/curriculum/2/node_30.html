Meet Ms. Numera

Hi! My name is Ms. Numera, and I will help you make scientific predictions based on mathematical evidence.

Let's use probability to measure how likely it is that an event will occur. Probabilities can be written as fractions, ratios, or percentages, and are often used in everyday life. For example, perhaps you have seen probabilities when the weather man gives a forecast: There is a 20% chance of rain today.


Probabilities can also be used in genetics to predict the chances that a child will be born with a certain trait.
But before we get in to the probability of genetics, I think it might be good to review the basics of probability with the flip of a coin!
