Calculating Inheritance Probabilities

In genetics, we use probability to predict the chance that two parents will have a child with a certain genotype. Geneticists use a tool called a Punnett Square (invented by Reginald C. Punnett in the early 1900s) to help them calculate these probabilities.



Image curtesy of http://www.dnaftb.org/dnaftb/concept_5/con5bio.html

In a Punnett square:
  • The Genotypes of the parents are found along the top and sides of the Punnett square.
  • All possible gene combinations for children born to these parents are represented inside the square.


A Punnett Square with Earlobe Alleles

Using the Data in the Punnett square to the left, you can translate your numbers into percentages for the probability of different genotypes.

1 out of 4 = 1/4 = 25%
2 out of 4 = 2/4 = 1/2 =50%
3 out of 4 = 3/4 = 75%
4 out of 4 = 4/4 = 1 = 100%
