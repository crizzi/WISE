A Simple Inheritance Model

When we know the genotypes of the parents, it is possible to predict what the genotypes of their children will be.


Because it is harder to study this in real life, we will use the model to see how alleles are donated by parents to make different genotypes of their child.
Consider while using the model:

  • What happens when you vary the genotypes of the parents? Try this for the parental crosses EE x ee, Ee x Ee, Ee x ee.
  • Why do you think there needs to be so many test offspring?
  • Is there a pattern to the way alleles are donated from each parent?

Record your results on a SEPARATE SHEET OF PAPER, record. You'll need them for the next step.

To change the parents' genotypes, click on one or both of the genes for each parent in the model. When you click START, the model will show you ten sample genotypes of the children.

Then click the start button.
