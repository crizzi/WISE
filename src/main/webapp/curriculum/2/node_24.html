To understand how Eric got his cystic fibrosis, we need to understand how traits are inherited.

Traits are features that are passed on from generation to generation. We inherit traits from our parents, and pass them on to our children.

  

There are THREE kinds of inherited traits: 

  1. Physical traits include, eye color, blood type, and height.

  2. Behavioral traits are the ways one acts. For example, a sheepdog's herding instinct is a trait that it inherited from its parents.
  3. Predispositions to medical conditions are traits that give one an increased risk of getting certain diseases. For example, heart disease, cancer, and mental illness are all diseases that can run in a family.

I have summarized the information that you gathered from Ms. Codon about the inheritance of an observable trait below:

  • Genes are portions of DNA that can be inherited from ones parents.
  • Genes for a trait can have different forms, called alleles, that result in different phenotypes of the trait.
  • These alleles can be dominant or recessive alleles.
  • Two genes, one from each parent, are needed for a trait to occur in an individual.
  • The two-allele combination that is inherited from one's parents is called a genotype.
