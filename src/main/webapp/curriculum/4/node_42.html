What do greenhouse gases do to energy?
As you saw in the model, some outward moving IR radiation is absorbed by carbon dioxide (#1 below) and then re-emitted back down towards Earth (#2 below). This re-emitted IR warms the Earth. In reality, many gases do this besides carbon dioxide (such as methane and water vapor). These gases are called greenhouse gases because they help keep the Earth warm. If we didn't have greenhouse gases the Earth's temperature would be very cold. In fact, all of the Earth's water would freeze solid!

But what happens when too much greenhouse effect occurs? This is called global climate change, because having too much greenhouse gas in the atmosphere can cause dramatic changes in climate. In reality, there are many factors that contribute to changes in the climate.
