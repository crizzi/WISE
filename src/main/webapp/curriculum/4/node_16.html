Combusting hydrocarbons in air
You now know that substances react with each other in specific ratios. But you might have guessed that in real life, substances do not always come together in these perfect amounts! What happens then?

In reality, hydrocarbons are often burned while being SURROUNDED by air--that is, in an EXCESS of oxygen. Therefore, how much hydrocarbon there is LIMITS how much product is produced. It is called the LIMITING REACTANT.
