Reactants combine in specific ratios
Chemical reactions occur in much the same way as assembling a bicycle. When burning a hydrocarbon such as methane (CH4), ONE molecule of methane combines with EXACTLY TWO molecules of oxygen (O2) to form ONE molecule of carbon dioxide (CO2) and TWO molecules of water (H2O), plus some heat energy.




Notice that the numbers of atoms of C, H, and O are the same on both sides of the arrow
(just like assembling bicycles)!
