Option A: Increasing Renewable Energy 20% by 2020
Coal-burning power plants are the largest U.S. source of carbon dioxide pollution -- they produce 2.5 billion tons every year. These power plants give us electricity for our homes.

Renewable energy, such as solar and wind power, are renewable because sources like the Sun and wind will not run out. They also do not produce carbon dioxide like coal-burning power plants. We can reduce the amount of carbon dioxide that power plants produce by urging congress to make electricity suppliers increase their use of renewable energy to 20% by 2020. Many states have already adopted this standard.
